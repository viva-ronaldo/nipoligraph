About

Data are updated roughly every week, using sources:

  • Assembly details from the official NI Assembly site; in particular, the AIMS Portal
  • Tweets from Twitter, gathered using rtweet
  • News articles from NewsRiver
  • Polling data from any relevant source, which for Northern Ireland is almost exclusively LucidTalk

Plots are created with Altair, a Python interface to Vega-Lite. Other displays use a few JavaScript/jQuery libraries, including DataTables, Sparklines, ProgressPieSVG, and autoComplete. The data science parts of the site use a little , and more Python: NumPy, pandas, Gensim, NLTK, and scikit-learn.

The site is built with Python and Flask and is hosted on .

Original artwork is by Ellistration.

Other sites in this niche to check out:

  • They Work For You: includes full Plenary debate proceedings
  • The Public Whip: detailed voting data for the UK Parliament
  • Vote View: UCLA site covering votes in the USA; uses a similar method of positioning members of Congress in space based on their voting record
  • PolitWoops: records tweets that are posted and deleted
